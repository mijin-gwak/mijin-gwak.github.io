Optimizing Keyword Spotting in Automatic
Speech Recognition


Tools Used Python
Skills Used Machine Learning

Delve into the unexplored realm of determining the optimal amount of training materials for keyword spotting in automatic speech recognition. This study employs a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) model, analyzing critical points such as global maximum, global minimum, local maxima, and local minima to identify the most effective training dataset size. Utilizing the Google Commands Dataset with 10 selected words, this research aims to answer the fundamental question: 'How many training materials are sufficient for achieving optimal accuracy in keyword spotting?

Analyzing Children's
First Words


Tools Used R
Skills Used Data Analysis,
Data Visualization, Statistics

Delve into the world of early childhood vocabulary development with my study, titled "Say Mama" Children's linguistic journey is a fascinating exploration marked by both quantitative growth and selective acquisition of words. Despite being exposed to hundreds to thousands of words per hour, their initial vocabulary is a fraction of this input. Previous research suggests that input frequency plays a pivotal role in this selectivity. Just as a child's first words in English often include "mama" or "dada" due to frequent parental usage, this thesis aims to examine the parental input frequency of individual Korean words and its correlation with the age of acquisition.
